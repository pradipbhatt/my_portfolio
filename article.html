First Semester Journey: Exploring the World of Computer Engineering

In our first semester of computer engineering at Far Western University, we delved into a wide range of subjects, including Basic Electrical Engineering (BEE), Physics, Mathematics-I, and even a drawing workshop. It was an exciting time as I embarked on my journey into the world of web technologies and programming.

Learning HTML and the Influence of a Friend:

During this semester, my friend Pyush inspired me to learn HTML, the foundational language of the web. I was captivated by the idea of creating and designing web pages. Motivated by his encouragement, I immersed myself in learning HTML, grasping its concepts and exploring its vast possibilities.

Coding in C and GitHub Repository:

Alongside my HTML journey, I also began coding in the C programming language. I was intrigued by its power and versatility. To hone my skills and track my progress, I started a daily coding practice, writing and storing my C programs in a GitHub repository named "c-programs." GitHub became a valuable platform for showcasing my coding projects and collaborating with others.

A Memorable Journey Near Linga:

As the first semester drew to a close, we embarked on a memorable journey near Linga, immersing ourselves in the beauty of nature and strengthening our bonds as classmates. It was a time of reflection and celebration, marking the end of a successful semester and the beginning of an exciting future in computer engineering.

Second Semester: Exploring Coding and Teaching

As computer engineering students, we entered the second semester with high expectations of coding and game development classes, anticipating advanced lessons in cutting-edge technologies. However, we soon realized that coding and teaching were two different realms.

While our respected teacher excelled in teaching Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) concepts, we found that the practical classes were not as effective as we had hoped. However, I did not solely rely on the teacher's instruction. Through my daily learning habits and consistent efforts, I cultivated a strong grasp of OOP concepts. I dedicated time each day to coding in C++ to practice examples and enhance my logical thinking.

To keep track of my progress and showcase my coding achievements, I stored all my coding records in a GitHub repository named "OOP-programs." GitHub served as a valuable platform for organizing and sharing my projects with others.

In addition to the core subjects of coding and OOP, we also studied additional subjects such as Applied Mechanics, Mathematics-II, Chemistry, and Discrete Structures. Despite the challenges, we managed to navigate through the semester smoothly. However, amidst the ongoing pandemic, we experienced a transition to online classes, adapting to new modes of learning and collaboration.

Throughout this semester, I continued to develop my skills in web development. I created numerous web pages using HTML and delved deeper into CSS, enhancing my understanding of styling and design. Concurrently, my logical thinking and problem-solving abilities in programming languages, especially in implementing OOP concepts, showed significant improvement.

Final Project: Database Management System (DBMS)

As the culmination of our second semester, we were required to undertake a final project. For my project, I chose to develop a Database Management System (DBMS) using C++. This project involved submitting a proposal, which was reviewed and approved by our teacher. Once the proposal was accepted, we proceeded to the coding phase.

The project spanned several weeks, during which I dedicated myself to designing and implementing the DBMS. On the last day, we had a presentation to showcase our projects. I confidently delivered a compelling presentation and demonstrated the functionality and features of my DBMS project. I am proud to say that I received positive feedback and recognition for my presentation and the project itself.

The DBMS project, along with its documentation and source code, is available on my GitHub repository for anyone to explore and review.

Third Semester: Exploring Data Structures and Beyond

Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA)

In the third semester, we delved into the fascinating world of Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA). Our teacher, Abhishek Sir, stood out for his innovative teaching methods, emphasizing the latest tech visualization approaches to help us grasp complex concepts effectively.

Theory of Computation (TOC)

Alongside DSA, we studied Theory of Computation (TOC) under the guidance of Laxman Sir, fondly known as Laxxu Sir. We explored the theoretical aspects of computing, covering topics that expanded our understanding of computational models and languages.

Digital Logic (DL)

Another respected faculty member, Kamal Lekhak Sir, guided us through the intricacies of Digital Logic (DL). We delved into the fascinating world of flip-flops, decoders, and other fundamental building blocks of digital circuits.

Final Project: Digital Clock Simulation

As part of our final project for the semester, I developed a digital clock simulation using a simulator software. The project involved implementing a digital clock using a 555 timer. With careful design and coding, I created a fully functional digital clock simulation that accurately displayed the time.

After completing the project, we submitted a comprehensive project report to showcase our work. The project, along with its documentation and source code, is available on my GitHub repository for anyone interested in exploring it.

Mathematics-III and Electronic Circuits Theory(ECT)

In addition to the core subjects, we also had Mathematics-III and Electronic Circuits Theory (ECT). Bishesh Shah Sir, our Mathematics-III instructor, had a remarkable teaching style that focused on numerical problem-solving. His habit of assigning a lot of assignments helped us strengthen our mathematical skills. Moreover, his punctuality and ability to complete the course on time were highly appreciated by the students.

Memorable Refreshment Trip

At the end of the semester, we embarked on a memorable journey to Karnali Bridge and Tikapur for refreshment. The trip provided us with an opportunity to relax, bond with our classmates, and create wonderful memories that we will cherish forever.

Fourth Semester Events: A Journey of Empowerment and Collaboration

During my fourth semester, I had the incredible opportunity to organize and conduct a series of events that aimed to empower students and foster collaboration within the field of technology. These events not only enriched my own learning experience but also provided a platform for fellow students to enhance their skills and knowledge.

Canvas Design Event:

To kickstart the semester, I organized a two-day event focused on Canva design. Canva is a powerful graphic design tool that enables users to create visually stunning and professional designs. Through this event, participants were introduced to the fundamentals of Canva and learned how to leverage its features to create eye-catching designs. It was an engaging and hands-on experience that allowed students to explore their creativity and develop their design skills.

Git and GitHub Event:

Recognizing the importance of version control and collaborative coding, I conducted a two-day event dedicated to Git and GitHub. Git is a distributed version control system, while GitHub is a web-based platform that facilitates collaboration and code sharing. During this event, participants gained practical knowledge of Git's key functionalities, such as creating branches, merging code, and resolving conflicts. Additionally, they learned how to leverage GitHub's features to contribute to open-source projects and collaborate with other developers effectively.

C Programming Language Bootcamp:

In continuation of my commitment to promoting technical skills, I organized a one-week bootcamp focused on the C programming language. C is a foundational language in computer science and provides a strong basis for understanding programming concepts. The bootcamp offered participants an immersive learning experience, covering topics such as variables, loops, functions, and data structures. Through hands-on coding exercises and projects, students gained confidence in their programming abilities and built a solid foundation in C.

Collaborative Learning and Empowerment:

These events were not just about acquiring technical knowledge but also about fostering collaboration and empowerment within the student community. By organizing these events, I aimed to create an environment where students could come together, share their experiences, and learn from one another. It was truly inspiring to witness the enthusiasm and passion that participants brought to each event, as they connected with like-minded individuals and expanded their horizons.

4th Semester End :

My fourth semester was marked by a series of events that focused on empowering students and fostering collaboration. From exploring the world of design through the Canva design event to delving into version control and collaboration with Git and GitHub, and finally, equipping students with essential programming skills through the C programming language bootcamp, these events provided a platform for growth, learning, and community building. I am grateful for the opportunity to have organized and conducted these events, and I look forward to continuing my journey of empowerment and collaboration in the field of technology.
